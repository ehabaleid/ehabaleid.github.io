Energy Efficiency of BEVs(Battery Electric Vehicles)🔋🚗

In this second scene you can explore the average city/highway/combined MPGe (Miles Per Gallon equivalent) of BEV vehicles by vehicle classification.
Since electric vehicles don't use gasoline, the EPA came up with the unit of measurement for the electric vehicles energy consumption level to compare with gas-powered vehicles.
The formula for MPGe is calculated as 33.7 kWh of electricity = 1 gallon of gasoline.

Select a radio button to see a different view for city or highway average MPG

BEVs are more efficient in low speed stop and go city type driving because they don't have an ICE idling and wasting energy every time you come to a stop at an intersection.
Furthermore regenerative braking helps recoup some energy back in stop and go traffic

The fuel economy data for this visualization is based off 2022 model year vehicles sourced from the US Department of Energy
